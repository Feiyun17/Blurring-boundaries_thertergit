BOUNDARIES —
THE TYPOLOGY OF THEATER

STAGE PLAY

Traditional theater establishes a clear boundary between actor and audience. The “fourth wall” remains invisible yet structurally impenetrable.

IMMERSIVE THEATRE

Boundaries are blurred as the audience moves through space, sharing a continuous physical environment with the performers.

CINEMA

The screen operates as a visual threshold. Physical separation persists, while perception is projected into a constructed reality.

BUSKING

Boundaries are temporary and permeable. The street becomes the stage, forming an ephemeral circle of performance.
